Welcome

I like to say that the monsoon has been my lifelong research collaborator—sometimes generous, sometimes unpredictable, but always fascinating. My research journey has taken me from mathematics to atmospheric science, leading me through IIT Kanpur, the Indian Institute of Science, Florida State University, IITM Pune, and now IIT Bhubaneswar. Along the way, I have been driven by a simple question: what governs the remarkable variability of the tropical climate system, and how can we predict it more reliably?

My research focuses on tropical climate dynamics, monsoon variability, air–sea interaction, and Earth system modeling, with particular emphasis on intraseasonal oscillations, extreme rainfall, and climate predictability. I combine physical theory, observational analyses, numerical simulations, and increasingly, interpretable artificial intelligence to understand processes operating across timescales ranging from weeks to decades.

I have been fortunate to receive the INSA Medal for Young Scientists and the Roddam Family Medal for the best Ph.D. thesis at IISc. At IIT Bhubaneswar, I enjoy working with students and collaborators to tackle challenging problems in climate science while developing computational tools that make research more reproducible, accessible, and impactful.
